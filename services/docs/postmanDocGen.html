MOTION TO DISMISS

Pursuant to Rule 12(b)(6) of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Defendant Marlowe Novarro (\"Mr. Novarro\") respectfully moves this Court for an order dismissing the claims against him in the above-captioned case in their entirety and with prejudice.

MEMORANDUM IN SUPPORT OF MOTION TO DISMISS

This action arises is an alleged violation of Plaintiff Jersey Jackson (\"Mr. Jackson\")'s First Amendment Rights. Mr. Novarro, an officer of the Rowe Police Department, is entitled to qualified immunities because they as a law enforcement officer, acting within the scope of his duties during the relevant incidents, and there are no allegations to overcome this defense.

According to the affidavits submitted by the parties, including Mr. Buchanan, Smith, Saab, Jackson, and Mr. Novarro himself, as well as the Rowe Police Department's Crowd Control Policy (Exhibit 2), Mr. Novarro was following guidelines and procedures set forth by the department. Mr. Novarro differentiated between individuals engaged in criminal behavior and those lawfully demonstrating, as required by the policy.

Furthermore, Mr. Novarro took the necessary precautions to ensure the safety of other individuals by asking demonstrators to disperse twice before making any arrests. This action is in accordance with the specific procedures outlined in the Crowd Control Policy (Exhibit 2).

To surpass the defense of qualified immunity, Mr. Jackson must establish that Mr. Novarro violated clearly established statutory or constitutional rights, of which a reasonable person would have known. Here, Mr. Jackson has failed to allege facts to support such a violation or that a reasonable officer in Mr. Novarro's position would have known of it.

As such, all claims against Mr. Novarro should be dismissed based on qualified immunity.
