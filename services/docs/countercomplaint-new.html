Counter-Complaint

Defendant and Counter-Plaintiff Marlowe Navarro, an officer of the Rowe Police Department ("Navarro"), brings this Counter-Complaint against Plaintiff and Counter-Defendant Jersey Jackson ("Jackson"), seeking damages and other relief due to the frivolous claim of retaliation filed against Navarro.

Parties

1. Navarro is an individual employed as a police officer by the Rowe Police Department (RPD) and a resident of Rowe, Oregon. At all material times herein, Navarro was acting within the course and scope of their employment with RPD.

2. Jackson is an individual who resides in Rowe, Oregon. Jackson has filed a complaint alleging retaliatory arrest against Navarro.

Statement of Facts

3. Jackson, a journalist employed by The Rowegonian, attended a public demonstration on January 15, 2020, and was subsequently arrested by Navarro due to noncompliance with lawful orders to disperse.

4. Jackson alleges that Navarro acted unlawfully and retaliated against them for exercising their First Amendment rights.

5. Navarro contends that Jackson's arrest was lawful and based on Jackson's refusal to follow lawful orders.

Claims for Relief

First Claim for Relief: Frivolous Lawsuit

6. Navarro realleges and incorporates by reference the preceding paragraphs.

7. Jackson's claim against Navarro constitutes a frivolous lawsuit seeking to harass and cause unnecessary expense to Navarro.

8. Navarro has suffered damages as a result of this frivolous lawsuit, including costs and disbursements incurred in defending this action.

Prayer for Relief

WHEREFORE, Navarro respectfully requests the Court enter judgment in their favor and against Jackson as follows:

a) Dismissal of Jackson's complaint in its entirety;

b) An award of damages in an amount to be determined at trial;

c) An award of costs and disbursements incurred in defending this action; and

d) Such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.
